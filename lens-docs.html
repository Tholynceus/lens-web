Docs/Introduction

Welcome to LENS

LENS is a Chrome extension that injects on-chain intelligence directly into Twitter/X profiles. See a dev's trust score, Bankrbot tokens, fee activity, and community tags without ever leaving the page.

iLENS currently supports Base chain only. Multi-chain support is on the roadmap.

What is LENS?

When you open any Twitter/X profile, LENS automatically scans the bio for wallet addresses, contract addresses, and GitHub links then pulls real on-chain data and displays it directly under the profile.

No tab switching. No copy-pasting addresses into block explorers. Everything you need to evaluate a token dev is shown instantly, including a 0-100 trust score based on real on-chain behaviour.

Docs/Dev Claim Fee

Dev Claim Fee

Track every time a developer claims fees from their token contract on Base chain. One of the most powerful sell signals in crypto.

!Dev claim fee tracking is available for Base chain only in v1.0.0.

How it works

Most ERC-20 tokens on Base include a fee mechanism. When trades happen, a small percentage goes to the developer's contract. The dev can then call a claimFees() or similar function to withdraw accumulated fees to their wallet.

LENS monitors all such transactions on Base. When a wallet in someone's bio has claimed fees, LENS displays the full history inline.

What LENS shows

FieldDescription
Total ClaimsHow many times fees have been claimed across all contracts
Total AmountValue of all fees claimed
Last ClaimWhen the most recent claim occurred
DestinationWhere claimed funds were sent wallet or CEX
!If claimed funds go directly to a known CEX address, this is a strong sell signal. The dev is likely converting fees to fiat.

Docs/PleaseBro Detection

PleaseBro Detection

Detect Bankrbot fee-share relationships. See whether a dev is sharing fees with partners and which tokens they are receiving fees from.

How it works

On Bankrbot, a token can route a portion of its fees to another party, often a partner or collaborator. This is known as a PleaseBro relationship. LENS detects these relationships on-chain and shows them directly on the profile.

What LENS shows

FieldDescription
TokenThe token sharing fees with this profile
SharePercentage of fees routed to this recipient
StatusWhether fees are currently accruing

Docs/CA History

CA History

Track every contract address ever posted in a Twitter/X bio including ones that have been deleted.

How bio tracking works

LENS indexes Twitter/X bios daily. Every time a CA appears in a bio, it is logged with a timestamp. When the CA disappears from the bio, it is marked as "Deleted" but never removed from LENS records.

iBio snapshots are taken every 24 hours. There may be up to a 24-hour delay before a new CA appears in LENS history.

Docs/Cabal Wallet

Cabal Wallet

Map the connected wallets behind a dev and expose the hidden cluster of accounts that move together. See the real group, not just one face.

!Cabal Wallet is currently in active development and not yet live.

Why it matters

A single dev rarely operates from a single wallet. Teams and cabals often spread activity across many wallets that quietly fund each other, buy the same tokens, and receive fees from the same contracts. Looking at one address tells you almost nothing about the real operation behind it.

Cabal Wallet analyzes funding paths, shared counterparties, and coordinated transactions on Base to cluster wallets that are likely controlled by the same group. Instead of seeing one face, you see the whole network.

What it will show

FieldDescription
Connected WalletsAddresses likely controlled by the same entity
Funding LinksWhich wallets funded which, and when
Shared HoldingsTokens the cluster holds or accumulates together
Coordination SignalsBuys or sells that move in sync across the cluster
+Cabal Wallet helps surface insiders, coordinated pumps, and hidden teams before you trust a single profile.

Docs/Smart Follower

Smart Follower

See which notable on-chain players and KOLs actually follow a profile, so you can tell genuine traction from bought hype at a glance.

!Smart Follower is currently in active development and not yet live.

Why it matters

Follower counts are easy to fake. What is far harder to fake is who follows a profile. When real builders, known traders, and respected KOLs follow an account, that is a strong signal of genuine traction.

Smart Follower cross-references a profile's followers against a curated set of high-signal on-chain accounts and surfaces the notable ones, so you can instantly separate a real audience from bots and bought engagement.

Docs/Username History

Username History

See every username a Twitter/X account has ever used. Scammers frequently change handles to escape their reputation.

!Username History is currently in active development and not yet live.

How username tracking works

LENS maintains a historical index of Twitter/X usernames mapped to account IDs. When a username changes, the old username is preserved in LENS records permanently and chained to the current handle.

This means even if someone changes their handle, anyone viewing their profile through LENS will see their full name history, so a fresh rebrand can never hide a shady past.

Docs/FAQ

FAQ

Frequently asked questions about LENS.

Is LENS free?v
Yes. The Chrome extension is completely free. An API tier will be available for developers who want programmatic access to LENS data.
Which chains does LENS support?v
LENS currently supports Base chain for all on-chain features. Ethereum, BSC, and Arbitrum are on the roadmap for future releases.
Does LENS access my wallet or private keys?v
No. LENS never asks for wallet access or private keys. It is a read-only tool that only reads public on-chain data and public Twitter/X profile information.
What features are coming soon?v
Cabal Wallet (mapping connected wallets behind a dev), Smart Follower (notable accounts that follow a profile), GitHub Intel (repo verification), and Username History (handle change tracking) are all in active development.
How often is data updated?v
On-chain data is updated in near real-time via blockchain indexing. Bio and CA history snapshots are taken every 24 hours. Community tags are updated instantly.
